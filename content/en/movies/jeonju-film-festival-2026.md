The 27th Jeonju International Film Festival (JIFF) opened on July 2 with a record 147 Korean independent films in its program, signaling a robust recovery for the country's
independent film sector after several challenging years. The 10-day festival, held across venues in Korea's cultural capital of Jeonju, has drawn over 80,000 attendees in its
first week, exceeding pre-pandemic attendance figures.

This year's festival introduced a new competitive section, "Digital Dreams," dedicated to films that incorporate artificial intelligence in their production process. The section
has already sparked controversy and conversation, with eight selected films using generative AI tools for everything from script development to visual effects. Festival director
Cho Young-jung defended the inclusion during the opening press conference. "Independent cinema has always been where artists experiment with new tools," she said. "We cannot
afford to ignore the most disruptive technological change in filmmaking since digital cameras."

## Breakout Films and Emerging Voices

The Korean Competition section has yielded several discoveries. "Concrete Heart," a debut feature by 28-year-old director Kim Su-bin, has emerged as the festival's breakout hit,
winning the Audience Award following its sold-out premiere. The film, shot on a budget of only ₩150 million, follows a retired ajumma who becomes an underground kickboxer to save
her community center from demolition. Critics have praised its raw energy and the performance of veteran character actress Youn Yuh-jung in the lead role.

Other highlights include "The Translator," a tense political thriller set during the 1980 Gwangju Uprising told from the perspective of a Japanese interpreter, and "Mono No
Aware," a contemplative Korean-Japanese co-production about a failing sake brewery in Busan's Japantown. The latter won the Best Cinematography award from the international jury,
chaired by Iranian director Asghar Farhadi.

JIFF continues through July 12, with the competitive awards announced at the closing ceremony.
