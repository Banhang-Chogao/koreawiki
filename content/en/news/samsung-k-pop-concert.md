Samsung Electronics will co-host what industry insiders are calling the largest corporate-sponsored K-pop event in history at Busan Asiad Stadium on August 15, Korea's Liberation
Day. The Samsung Hope Concert will feature headliners including BTS member SUGA, NCT 127, Seventeen, and rising rookie group RIIZE, with all proceeds benefiting youth education
programs in underserved communities across Gyeongsangnam-do.

The partnership, brokered over six months between Samsung's CSR division and the two-largest K-pop entertainment conglomerates, represents a strategic shift in how Korean chaebol
engage with popular culture. "This isn't a product launch disguised as a concert," said Samsung VP Park Jae-won during Thursday's press conference in Busan. "We are investing in
the cultural fabric of this nation and the future of its young people." The company has committed ₩15 billion to the initiative, with ₩10 billion going directly to scholarship
funds.

## A New Model for Corporate-Artist Collaboration

Industry analysts note that the structure of the Samsung Hope Concert differs significantly from traditional corporate sponsorships. Artists will retain full creative control over
their performances, and a portion of merchandise sales will be donated to causes chosen by each act. Seventeen has already announced that their share will support performing arts
programs in provincial schools, while SUGA's contribution will fund music production scholarships.

Tickets are being distributed through a lottery system rather than traditional sales, with 40,000 of the 55,000 available seats allocated to students, teachers, and community
members nominated by local education offices. The remaining seats will go to a public lottery open to all Korean residents. International fans will have access to a live-stream
broadcast on Samsung's streaming platform, with Korean and English subtitles provided.

Busan Metropolitan City has pledged additional security and transportation support, expecting tens of thousands of visitors to the coastal city for the event. Local hotels and
guesthouses in the Haeundae district have reported near-capacity bookings for the week of the concert.
