HYBE Corporation reported record second-quarter earnings of ₩620 billion on Friday, surpassing analysts' consensus estimates by 8% and sending shares up 5.3% in afternoon trading
on the Korea Exchange. The conglomerate, home to BTS, Seventeen, and LE SSERAFIM, attributed the strong performance to a packed global touring schedule and accelerating commerce
revenue from its Weverse platform.

Operating profit reached ₩135 billion, a 28% increase from the same period last year, driven by the consolidated touring revenue of BTS members currently on solo world tours,
including SUGA's "D-Day" stadium tour and Jung Kook's North American arena run. Seventeen's ongoing "Follow Again" tour, which kicked off in Seoul in March and has since moved
through Japan and Southeast Asia, contributed an estimated ₩95 billion in ticket and merchandise sales alone.

## Weverse Emerges as a Profit Center

Weverse, HYBE's fan community platform, saw monthly active users grow to 12.8 million, generating ₩87 billion in revenue from digital merchandise, exclusive content subscriptions,
and concert live-streaming. The company has invested heavily in AI-powered personalization features for the platform, including real-time translation for live streams in 12
languages.

CEO Park Ji-won stated during the earnings call that HYBE is "exploring strategic acquisitions in the Latin American and European markets" to diversify beyond its Asian
stronghold. The company also confirmed that plans for a new girl group under its subsidiary ADOR are on track for a debut in early 2027, following the successful trajectory of
NewJeans under the same label.

Analysts at Daishin Securities maintained their "Buy" rating on HYBE stock, raising their target price to ₩310,000, citing the company's successful monetization of its multi-label
structure and global reach.
