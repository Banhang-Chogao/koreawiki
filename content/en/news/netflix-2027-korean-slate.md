Netflix Korea announced its most ambitious content slate to date on Tuesday, committing ₩1.5 trillion to 28 original Korean productions scheduled for release in 2027. The
announcement, made at a star-studded event at the COEX Convention Center in Samseong-dong, positions Korea as the streaming giant's single largest content investment market
outside of the United States.

The slate includes the much-anticipated third season of "Squid Game," which begins production in September under director Hwang Dong-hyuk's renewed creative partnership with
Netflix. Also announced was a yet-untitled romance drama by Kim Eun-sook, the legendary screenwriter behind "Guardian: The Lonely and Great God" and "The King: Eternal Monarch,"
marking her first collaboration with the platform.

## Breaking New Ground in Korean Genre Cinema

Perhaps the most eye-catching announcement was "Last Orbit," described as Korea's first big-budget space thriller. The film, directed by "Train to Busan" alum Lee Sang-woo and
starring Song Kang-ho, will feature visual effects by Weta Workshop. With a reported budget of ₩80 billion, it represents a significant bet on Korean cinematic storytelling's
ability to compete with Hollywood on the global stage.

Netflix also announced a continued commitment to reality and variety programming, including a second season of the global phenomenon "Physical: 100" and a new culinary competition
series hosted by Baek Jong-won, titled "The Last Banchan." The company reported that Korean content accounted for 12% of global viewing hours on the platform in the first half of
2026, up from 8% in 2025.

Kang Min-kyung, CEO of Netflix Korea, emphasized the company's focus on creative independence. "Our role is to provide resources and a global platform," she said. "The stories,
the talent, and the vision are entirely Korean."
