Mnet's "Street Woman Fighter" returns for its third season on July 25 with a format overhaul designed to capitalize on the franchise's growing international appeal. The dance
competition, which launched in 2021 and became a cultural phenomenon, has expanded from eight Korean crews to 12, including three international crews from Japan, France, and the
United States.

The production budget for Season 3 is reportedly ₩50 billion — more than double the previous season and the largest ever for a Korean variety program. The investment is visible in
the new set, a 3,000-square-meter space in Paju that features a main stage equipped with 360-degree LED screens, a hydraulic floor system, and a dedicated 400-seat audience area.
"We wanted to create a performance environment that rivals a world tour," said creative director Kang Yeon-jung.

## International Competitors Raise the Stakes

The inclusion of international crews has added a new layer of tension. The Japanese crew "Tokyo Renegades" arrived with a reputation forged in Japan's underground dance scene,
while the French crew "Les Royaux" brings a hip-hop foundation that differs markedly from Korean dance styles. The American crew "Bronx Storm" features members who have danced for
Beyoncé and Janet Jackson.

"I have watched Seasons 1 and 2 obsessively," said Bronx Storm captain Maya Thompson during the press conference. "Korean dance crews are technically incredible. But we bring a
different energy — raw, street-born, unpolished. I think the collision of styles will make incredible television."

The judging panel has also been refreshed, with new judges including international choreographer Parris Goebel and BTS's j-hope, who served as a judge in Season 2. "Dance is a
universal language," j-hope said. "This season, more than ever, we will see that language spoken in many different dialects."
