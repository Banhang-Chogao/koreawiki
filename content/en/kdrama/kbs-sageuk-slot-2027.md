KBS announced its 2027 historical drama lineup on Tuesday, confirming three major sageuk productions that the public broadcaster hopes will reclaim its legacy as the home of
Korean historical storytelling. The slate includes "The Last Admiral," a 24-episode drama about Admiral Yi Sun-sin's final campaign; "Goryeo Dreams," a romance set during the
Goryeo dynasty; and "The Secret Annals," a court intrigue thriller centered on the Joseon royal archives.

The announcement comes as KBS has been losing ground in the drama wars to cable channels and streaming platforms, which have dominated ratings and cultural conversation in recent
years. KBS Drama Center director Lee Jae-moon acknowledged the challenge during the press conference at the broadcaster's Yeouido headquarters. "KBS built the sageuk genre," he
said. "We invented the template. Now is the time to reinvent it."

## Ambitious Casting and Production

"The Last Admiral" has already generated the most buzz, with veteran actor Choi Min-sik confirmed to play the iconic Joseon naval commander. The drama, written by "Six Flying
Dragons" scribe Kim Young-hyun, will focus on the final three years of Yi Sun-sin's life, culminating in the Battle of Noryang. With a budget of ₩50 billion, it will feature
large-scale naval battle sequences shot in a custom-built water tank at KBS's production facility in Suwon.

"Goryeo Dreams," meanwhile, takes a more romantic approach to history, following a Goryeo scholar (played by Rowoon) who falls in love with a Mongol princess (played by Bae Suzy)
during the Yuan intervention period. The drama aims to attract the younger demographic that has largely abandoned KBS's weekend dramas, incorporating a contemporary soundtrack and
modern cinematic techniques.

"The Secret Annals" rounds out the lineup with a darker, more suspenseful story. Set during the reign of King Yeonsangun, it follows a team of royal scribes who discover a
conspiracy hidden within the official records. Lee Joon-gi is in talks for the lead role of the chief scribe.

All three dramas are slated for 2027 broadcasts, with "The Last Admiral" expected to premiere in March.
