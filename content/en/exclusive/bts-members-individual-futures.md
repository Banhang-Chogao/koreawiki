Over the course of four months, KoreaWiki sat down individually with all seven members of BTS — Jin, SUGA, j-hope, RM, Jimin, V, and Jung Kook — for a series of conversations about their lives, their solo work, and the future of the group that changed K-pop forever. What emerged is a portrait of seven young men at peace with their past and thoughtful about what comes next.

The interviews took place between March and June of 2026, in locations ranging from SUGA's studio in Hannam-dong to a café in Itaewon where RM holds a weekly book club. Each member spoke candidly about the journey from the group's 2018 pivot to the West through their 2022 hiatus announcement and into the present chapter of individual careers.

## On the Reunion

"I get asked about this every single day," said RM, speaking in English for much of our conversation. "And the honest answer is: we don't have a date. We don't have a deadline. What we have is a promise. The seven of us made a promise to each other, and we're not going to break it."

Jin, who completed his military service in 2024 and has since released two solo albums and launched a variety show on SBS, was more cryptic. "I know something that the others don't know," he said with a laugh. "But I've been sworn to secrecy. What I can tell ARMY is: don't worry. We are not done. The story is not finished."

SUGA, ever the pragmatist, framed the question in practical terms. "BTS will reunite when the music demands it," he said. "Not when the calendar demands it, not when the industry demands it, not when the stock price demands it. When we have something to say that can only be said as seven people. That is the only condition."

j-hope, who completed his military service in 2025 and has since established himself as a Lollapalooza headliner and fashion collaborator, emphasized the importance of the solo chapter. "We needed to become individuals so that we could come back as something stronger. The BTS of 2026 would not have been the same if we had never taken this break."

Jimin, V, and Jung Kook — who served together in the military and have remained in near-daily contact — spoke about the deep bonds that military service reinforced. "I was scared that we would grow apart," Jimin admitted. "But actually, spending time apart made us appreciate each other more. We text every day. We send each other music. We are still BTS. We are just BTS in a different form right now."

No date has been announced for a group reunion, but multiple members hinted at "significant conversations" scheduled for early 2027.
