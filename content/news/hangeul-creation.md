## Overview

Hangeul (한글) is the Korean alphabet, created in 1443 under King Sejong the Great. It is widely regarded as the most scientifically designed writing system in the world.

## Historical Context

Before Hangeul, Koreans used Classical Chinese characters (Hanja). This meant only the educated elite could read and write. King Sejong sought to create a script accessible to all Koreans.
